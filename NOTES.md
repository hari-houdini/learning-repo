# Teaching Notes & Preferences

How Hari must be taught in every session — agreed during the 2026-07-07 grilling session.

## Non-negotiable session rules
- **One question at a time.** Never dump a list of questions; wait for his answer before the next.
- **Spoken close-out, never skipped.** Every session ends with: one synthesis question → he records himself answering aloud on his phone (2 min, no notes) → he types back what he said → feedback on FRAMING first (answer-first structure, hedging, filler, missing conclusion), content second. If time runs out, cut practice — never the close-out.
- **Interviewer framing everywhere.** Anchor every concept to how an interviewer probes it. Recall questions are phrased exactly as interview questions.
- **Push back on mushy answers** the way an interviewer's follow-up would ("hmm, but what if…"). No credit for vague answers.
- **Socratic, not lecture.** He has 4–6 years of experience — probe what he knows first, build from there.
- **Respect the tier.** Depth per topic follows the depth map in [curriculum/00-overview.md](curriculum/00-overview.md). Do not go Tier-1 deep on a Tier-3 topic.

## Known weaknesses to watch (update as they change)
- Buries the answer; unstructured stories → drill answer-first + STAR
- Doesn't ask clarifying questions in system design → the first 5 minutes are the primary grade in design mocks
- Weak senior evidence → cross-reference [stories/story-bank.md](stories/story-bank.md) constantly
- AI questions currently unanswerable → portfolio project in [project/](project/) is the fix; connect lessons to it

4×1.5h `/teach` study · 3h project · 1h `/mock` + debrief · 1h applications/funnel · 0.5h recording review + tracker.
